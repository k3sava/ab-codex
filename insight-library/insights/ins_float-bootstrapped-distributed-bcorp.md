# Float bootstrapped to 4,500+ customers as a globally distributed B Corp without venture capital

## Claim
Glenn Rogers and Lars Gelfan founded Float in 2011 inside a New York ad agency and grew it as a globally distributed remote team without outside investment. The product serves 4,500+ customers and 220,000+ scheduled people. The company is B Corp certified as of March 2024.

## Mechanism
A bootstrapped distributed team can absorb the steady customer-driven growth that a venture-backed equivalent would view as too slow. Profit per customer compounds because the team isn't subsidizing a category-leader sprint. B Corp certification adds an external accountability layer for people-first practices that founder-only commitment can't enforce alone.

## Conditions
Holds when the product solves a real operating problem (resource management for professional services) and the buyer rewards sustained quality over feature races.
Fails when the category collapses into platform consolidation where horizontal players bundle the workflow.

## Counter-evidence
Bootstrapped distributed scaling concedes the marketing budget needed to displace incumbents in adjacent categories. Float operates inside resource-management for agencies; expanding outside that vertical without outside capital would be slow.
