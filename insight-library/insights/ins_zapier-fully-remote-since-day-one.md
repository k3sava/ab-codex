# Zapier ran fully remote since day one and scaled to 800+ people across 38 countries without an office

## Claim
Wade Foster, Bryan Helmig, and Mike Knoop founded Zapier in 2011 and have operated fully remote since day one. The company is now 800+ people across 38 countries; the platform has automated 81 billion tasks for 2 million-plus businesses, including 69% of the Fortune 1000.

## Mechanism
Removing geographic constraint widens the hiring pool from "people who live within commute distance" to "people anywhere." A workflow-automation product fits remote-first culturally because the company is shipping the same kind of seam-removing tooling internally that it sells externally. Documentation discipline that remote work forces becomes the substrate that makes async communication possible.

## Conditions
Holds when the product is software-only (no on-site customer ops needed) and leadership commits to documented async-first decision-making.
Fails when the company is later forced to acquire on-site businesses or expand into regulated geographies that require physical presence.

## Signals

## Counter-evidence
Most "remote" companies inherit Zapier's structure but apply geo-cost-of-living adjustments to compensation, which produces the geo-arbitrage pattern the human-first doctrine rejects. Remote without geo-equity is cheaper labor, not better hiring.
