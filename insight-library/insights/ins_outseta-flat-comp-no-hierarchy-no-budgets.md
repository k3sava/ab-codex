# Outseta runs on flat comp, equity to all on founder terms, no hierarchy, no budgets, no sales team

## Claim
Geoff Roberts and Dimitris Karavias built Outseta as the canonical implementation of human-first comp at the price of personal salary. Same $210k salary for everyone with full flexibility, equity issued to every employee on the same terms as founders, no hierarchy, no budgets, no forecasts, no performance targets, no sales team. Roberts publicly states he forwent ~$500k in salary and benefits over five years to keep the model intact. He hopes the team stays at or below ~20 people indefinitely.

## Mechanism
Flat comp at a high baseline removes negotiation theater and pay inequities at the source. Equity on founder terms removes the two-tier (founder-equity vs option-grant) culture that produces founder-employee divergence at most startups. No budgets and no forecasts force the team to argue from first principles every quarter, which keeps the strategy honest. No sales team means the product carries the distribution. The structural choice that holds the model together is the founder accepting personal-salary concession during the build phase, in exchange for the durability of the operating principles for the team.

## Conditions
Holds when the founder has personal runway to accept below-market comp during the first 5+ years.
Fails when the category requires growth-by-headcount or when investor capital introduces equity-dilution-based comp pressure.

## Signals
- Same dollar salary in every offer letter, regardless of role.
- Equity grants in employee paperwork match founder-equity terms.
- No quarterly budget cycle, no annual performance review process.
- No SDR or AE on the org chart.

## Counter-evidence
The Outseta model is reproducible only when the founder accepts substantial personal-salary concession during the build phase. For founders without alternate income or partner support, forgoing $500k over five years isn't structurally feasible. The pattern also caps the company's effective scale at the team-size limit Roberts names (~20 people); categories that demand 50+ engineers can't be served this way.
