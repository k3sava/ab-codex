# Automattic has been distributed-first since 2005, scaling to 1,449 people across 81 countries

## Claim
Matt Mullenweg founded Automattic in 2005 and has run it distributed-first ever since. The company now employs 1,449 Automatticians across 81 countries, speaking 107 languages, predating the modern remote-work category by more than a decade.

## Mechanism
Distributed-first when "everyone-is-remote" is the founding rule produces written-communication discipline as a side effect, which scales well past where co-located teams hit coordination limits. The longer the distributed posture compounds, the more the operating manual (Automattic's Five Levels of Autonomy, P2 communication) becomes a self-reinforcing artifact that new hires absorb in onboarding.

## Conditions
Holds when leadership refuses to open offices that create two-tier (remote vs in-office) culture.
Fails when an acquired business comes with on-site staff who can't migrate to async-first work.

## Counter-evidence
Automattic's scale brings the same geo-arbitrage tension Buffer and Zapier carry. Distributed without explicit geo-equity in compensation drifts toward cheaper-talent hiring, even if individual roles publish ranges.
