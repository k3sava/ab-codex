# Quaderno hires customer support on a 5-hour workday, fully remote

## Claim
Carlos Hernández runs Quaderno on a 5-hour focused workday for support roles, advertising "work smarter, not harder" as the operating principle. The Spain-bootstrapped tax-compliance SaaS reached 1,000+ paying customers and ~$3.6M ARR over 12 years on this posture before being acquired by Visma in September 2023.

## Mechanism
Five hours of focused work captures most of the productive output of an 8-hour day once meetings, context-switching, and idle time are removed. Defining the role around 5 focused hours rather than 8 padded hours makes the comp ratio better for both sides: the company pays for high-value time only, and the contributor keeps the rest of their day for life. The pattern self-selects for people who can work without constant supervision and produce against outcomes, not against hours-warmed seats.

## Conditions
Holds when work can be defined by completed tasks (tickets resolved, customers helped) rather than by attendance.
Fails when role requires synchronous coverage windows that don't fit into 5-hour blocks across timezones.

## Signals
- Job listings advertise the hour cap as a feature.
- Roles are defined by output (resolved tickets, customer outcomes), not attendance.
- Founder publicly defends the discipline as a competitive advantage, not a perk.

## Counter-evidence
A 5-hour workday compresses synchronous overlap with global customers. For categories with high enterprise expectations of real-time coverage, the model shifts cost to either premium hourly rates or async-only customer expectations. Quaderno's category (tax compliance for online businesses) tolerates the async posture better than, say, real-time financial trading.
