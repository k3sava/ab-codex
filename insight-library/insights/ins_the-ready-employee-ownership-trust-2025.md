# The Ready converted to an Employee Ownership Trust in 2025, third structural-permanence pattern in the calm-company canon

## Claim
Aaron Dignan founded The Ready in late 2015 as a future-of-work consultancy serving Johnson & Johnson, Microsoft, Airbnb, Citibank, and others. The firm operated through 2024 and converted to an Employee Ownership Trust in early 2025. The structural choice joins Ghost (non-profit foundation) and Planning Center (Ministry Centered Foundation) as the third distinct legal mechanism in the lineage for ensuring a calm-company can't be sold against the team's interest.

## Mechanism
An Employee Ownership Trust holds the company on behalf of current and future employees. Unlike share-based ESOPs, the EOT structure removes the per-employee equity pool that creates exit pressure, and locates ownership in the trust itself. The mechanism mirrors Ghost's foundation and Planning Center's Ministry Centered Foundation: convert the company into a legal entity that cannot be acquired or sold against its purpose. Each pattern fits a different organizational shape (consultancy, software product, non-profit-adjacent SaaS), but the structural intent is identical.

## Conditions
Holds when the founder accepts forgoing the founder-equity exit in exchange for the durability of the operating principles.
Fails when capital-intensive growth requires investor liquidity expectations that an EOT can't accommodate.

## Evidence
The Ready operated 2015-2024 serving Johnson & Johnson, Microsoft, Airbnb, Citibank, Charles Schwab, Kaplan, Lloyds Bank, and others.

## Counter-evidence
Employee Ownership Trusts are jurisdiction-specific (UK has the cleanest EOT framework; US implementations are less standardized). The structure also locks the founder out of liquidity events that might otherwise fund reinvestment in adjacent bets. The EOT pattern works for service firms with stable revenue; it fits less well for capital-intensive product companies that need outside investment to scale distribution.
