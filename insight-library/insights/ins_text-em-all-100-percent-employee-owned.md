# Text-Em-All converted to 100% employee ownership; founder-led 18-year SaaS now owned by the team

## Claim
Brad Herrmann built Text-Em-All as a mass-texting SaaS over 18 years and converted the company to 100% employee ownership. The structural choice joins Ghost's non-profit foundation, Planning Center's Ministry Centered Foundation, and The Ready's Employee Ownership Trust as the fourth distinct legal mechanism in the calm-company canon for ensuring the company can't be sold against the team's interest. Trusted by 16,000+ organizations with 42 employees.

## Mechanism
100% employee ownership concentrates the long-term incentive on the people who do the work, rather than on outside shareholders or a founder-equity pool waiting for an exit. The mechanism removes the single-point-of-failure risk that kills calm companies on founder transitions: the company can't be acquired and reshaped, because the team owns the asset and the operating principles together. Compared to ESOPs (which distribute shares as compensation and create exit-pressure on retirement), 100% employee ownership locks the structural permanence into the cap table itself.

## Conditions
Holds when the founder is willing to convert their own equity stake into the employee-owned structure (often through a transition mechanism that pays the founder out over time from operating cashflow).
Fails when capital-intensive growth requires investor liquidity that an employee-owned structure can't accommodate.

## Evidence
Text-Em-All publicly states that the company is "a 100% employee-owned company" with 42 employees serving 16,000+ organizations. Founded by Brad Herrmann; 18-year operating history.

The structural family of permanence mechanisms in the calm-company lineage:
- **Ghost** (2013): non-profit foundation; legal constitution prevents sale
- **Planning Center** (2024): Ministry Centered Foundation; "will never be sold"
- **The Ready** (early 2025): Employee Ownership Trust conversion
- **Text-Em-All**: 100% employee ownership

Each mechanism solves the same problem (preventing acquisition against operating principles) with a different legal structure that fits the company's shape (open-source SaaS, faith-adjacent SaaS, consultancy, mass-texting SaaS).

## Signals
- 100% of equity held by employees and the trust mechanism, no founder-residual stake.
- Founder remains in operating role post-conversion (sustained leadership beyond the transaction).
- Customer-base scale (16,000+ orgs) sustained across the conversion.

## Counter-evidence
100% employee ownership is jurisdiction-specific in implementation; tax treatment varies by country, and US ESOP-vs-EOT distinctions affect the operating mechanics. The structure also requires a founder willing to forgo a traditional exit; founders motivated by liquidity targets are structurally incompatible.
