# Gumroad runs as 25 contractors with zero full-time employees, no meetings, no deadlines

## Claim
Gumroad operates with 25 contributors and zero full-time employees including the CEO. The company has no meetings and no deadlines. Team communication runs through GitHub, Notion, and Slack with a 24-hour response window. Features ship incrementally whenever a development version beats production.

## Mechanism
Async written communication forces clarity that synchronous meetings paper over. Removing deadlines removes the forcing function that produces low-quality work; instead, ship-when-better-than-production produces continuous improvement. Contractor-only structure removes salary-and-benefits coordination overhead while still buying high-judgment time.

## Conditions
Holds when contributors can write well and tolerate written-only feedback loops; the product must support incremental shipping (no big-bang launch dependency).
Fails when work requires synchronous calibration (pitching enterprise buyers, complex regulatory contexts) or when contributors lack the writing fluency for async-only.

## Signals
- Zero full-time employees.
- Calendar shows no recurring meetings.
- Communication artifacts (GitHub PRs, Notion docs) are dense, written, async.

## Counter-evidence
Gumroad's no-deadline model concedes timed market opportunities (regulatory deadlines, seasonal launches) where shipping by a date matters more than shipping the best version. Pure async also limits onboarding speed for contributors who haven't yet learned the writing discipline.
