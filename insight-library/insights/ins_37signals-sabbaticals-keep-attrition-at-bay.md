# 37signals uses sabbaticals as the retention mechanism against the industry's atrocious 18-month tenure baseline

## Claim
David Heinemeier Hansson cites the average tech-industry tenure of "an atrocious 18 months" as the problem 37signals' sabbatical program is designed to solve. The mechanism is concrete: paid time off the keyboard at predictable intervals (typically every 5 years) so the team retains people who would otherwise leave at the 18-month mark. The result is multi-decade tenure inside a single firm, which compounds into product depth no high-attrition competitor can match.

## Mechanism
The 18-month industry tenure is a self-fulfilling prophecy: companies that don't invest in retention lose people at 18 months, and the resulting attrition tax forces them to over-hire and under-train, which produces more attrition. Sabbaticals break the loop. Predictable paid time off resets the burnout cycle before it produces a resignation, and the 5-year cadence creates a tenure incentive (you only get the sabbatical if you stay) that competing offers can't match. The compounding benefit is institutional memory: a 10-year operator carries context no two 5-year operators can replicate.

## Conditions
Holds when the company is profitable enough to absorb periodic paid leave and has the operational discipline to keep work moving when key people are out.
Fails when team is small enough that any single absence breaks the operating cadence, or when customer-facing roles can't tolerate scheduled gaps.

## Evidence

· DHH, *Sabbaticals Keep Our Attrition at Bay*, world.hey.com/dhh, October 29, 2025

Companion DHH post:
> "You must pay yourself first by doing the work that actually means something to you."

· DHH, *Pay Yourself First*, October 4, 2025

37signals has run sabbaticals for over a decade. Average tenure at the company materially exceeds the industry baseline, though specific numbers are not publicly stated in the post.

## Signals
- Sabbatical policy documented and applied at predictable intervals.
- Average tenure measurably above 18 months at the firm level.
- Operational cadence designed to absorb scheduled multi-week absences without crisis.

## Counter-evidence
Sabbaticals require sustained profitability to fund. Pre-profit companies cannot offer paid multi-week leave without hurting cashflow, even if the longer-term retention benefit would compound. The mechanism also requires deep enough roles that institutional memory is the moat; for highly transactional or commodity work, sabbatical-backed tenure produces less differential value.
