# Buffer published a salary formula and every employee's exact pay since December 2013

## Claim
Buffer began publishing a complete salary table in December 2013, including names, teams, titles, locations, and exact pay per employee. The published formula combines a Buffer Benchmark (custom percentile applied to market data per role) with a Cost of Living Adjustment (90% Global, 100% High). Refreshed January 2024.

## Mechanism
Open formulas remove the negotiation gap that produces pay inequities at hire-time and review-time. Managers stop spending cycles defending comp because the formula does it. The cost of leaking the table is zero because everything is already public, and candidates self-select against negotiation theater.

## Conditions
Holds when leadership accepts that comp is a recruiting tool first and a confidentiality concern second.
Fails when an acquired team carries inherited comp asymmetries, or when leadership wants discretion to retain individuals above the formula.

## Evidence
> "Transparency is one of our core values. We've found that it builds trust, holds us accountable, and can push our industry forward."

· Buffer, *Open Salaries at Buffer*, buffer.com/salaries

Per-employee table includes names, teams, titles, roles, locations, and exact salaries. Compensation philosophy stated as Transparent, Simple, Fair, Generous, with intent to "pay above market."

## Signals
- Compensation table publicly accessible per employee.
- Formula published in full, not summarized.
- New hires reference the formula, not negotiation.

## Counter-evidence
Buffer's formula bakes in a geo-cost-of-living adjustment (90% Global, 100% High), which is the geo-arbitrage pattern an AI-native human-first doctrine explicitly rejects. Transparency without geo-equity pays the same human less for the same output based on where they live. Pay should track value created, not location.
