# PMM is infrastructure that makes other functions faster, not a launch gate they wait on

## Claim
The PMM operating model that scales through AI-native teams treats PMM as a substrate of reusable assets, briefs, and tooling that other functions self-serve from, not a serial gate where every launch waits on PMM bandwidth.

## Mechanism
A gate-shaped PMM team is sized by its launch queue and bottlenecks the org. A substrate-shaped PMM team is sized by the assets it produces — positioning canvases, ICP libraries, comparative-claim repositories, AEO mention-rate dashboards — that other functions consume on demand. Sales, CS, and product self-serve from the substrate, escalating only edge cases. The substrate compounds across launches; the gate model resets each time. AI-native tooling lets one PMM operator maintain a substrate that previously required a team.

## Conditions
Holds when:
- The org's launch volume exceeds what serial PMM gating can handle.
- PMM has the discipline to author reusable assets (not bespoke deliverables) and the political authority to refuse the gate role.
- Other functions trust the substrate enough to self-serve.

Fails when:
- The org is small enough that ad-hoc PMM still works (one PMM, one product line, low launch frequency).
- PMM lacks tooling/AI to maintain substrate at quality (the assets go stale).
- Stakeholders want bespoke white-glove PMM and treat self-serve as a quality cut.

## Evidence
> "PMM stops being the team you wait on and starts being the team that makes everyone else faster."

— Elena Verna and Gemma Casey-Swift, https://www.elenaverna.com/p/whos-got-time-for-product-marketing, 2026-04-26

Companion piece (https://www.elenaverna.com/p/your-product-has-a-new-user-its-not, same week) makes the parallel argument from the product side: agents are first-class users; products that build for human-only paths get bypassed.

## Signals
- Sales/CS pull from a positioning library or comparative-claim repo without filing a PMM ticket.
- Launch-blocking PMM tickets shrink as % of PMM time; substrate-authoring time grows.
- Net Promoter or trust score from internal stakeholders rises while PMM headcount holds flat or shrinks.

## Counter-evidence
For high-stakes launches (rebrands, category-defining moves, regulated categories), the gate model has real value — substrate-only PMM can ship inconsistent positioning across high-leverage moments. Teams that go fully substrate-mode without a gate for tier-A launches sometimes regret it.
