# Cursor reached $2B ARR through pure product-led adoption with no traditional sales motion

## Claim
Anysphere (founded 2022 by Michael Truell, Sualeh Asif, Aman Sanger, and Arvid Lunnemark) shipped Cursor as an AI-native code editor that crossed $1B ARR fastest in software history and surpassed $2B ARR by early 2026. Adoption ran on product alone: 40,000 NVIDIA engineers, half of the Fortune 500, 80%+ of Y Combinator portfolio companies. No outbound, no SDR teams, no field-sales-led motion at the bottom of the funnel.

## Mechanism
When a product makes a daily-use professional dramatically faster at their core craft, the artifact (the codebase they ship) carries the marketing. Engineers tell other engineers because the gap between with-Cursor and without-Cursor is observable inside thirty minutes. Sales appears only at enterprise tier where procurement requires a contract.

## Conditions
Holds when the product targets a daily-use professional and the adoption signal is observable inside a single session.
Fails when the buyer is a committee whose users don't get to choose their tools.

## Signals
- ARR curve outpacing every prior software category.
- Zero SDR / outbound team relative to user base.
- Enterprise contracts triggered by user demand, not field push.

## Counter-evidence
Cursor's category (AI for developers) is uniquely suited to product-led: the buyer and user are the same person. For categories where the buyer is several layers above the user (procurement-led enterprise software), pure product-led without any sales motion likely undertrades.
