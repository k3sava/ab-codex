# Planning Center created the Ministry Centered Foundation in 2024 to ensure it will never be sold

## Claim
Jeff Berg and Aaron Stewart founded Planning Center in 2006 from a Southern California church and ran it bootstrapped, founder-owned, and debt-free for 18 years. In 2024 they formalized the Ministry Centered Foundation, a legal structure that ensures the company "will never be sold." Currently 100,000+ active churches, 4 million users, 200+ employees, average tenure 5+ years, 82% remote.

## Mechanism
A founder-owned company can be sold by its founders. A foundation-owned company cannot. The Ministry Centered Foundation locks the customer-mission (serving churches) into a legal structure that survives the founders, removing the future risk that a successor leadership team could optimize for sale rather than mission. The 2024 formalization came at year 18 of the company, which is a useful pattern: the structure is named once the company has proven its operating model, not at founding when the principles are still aspirational.

## Conditions
Holds when the founders accept forgoing the personal-equity exit in exchange for the durability of the customer mission.
Fails when capital-intensive growth requires investor liquidity that a foundation structure can't accommodate.

## Signals
- Foundation incorporation (verifiable corporate filing).
- Mission-aligned customer base (churches) acts as a test of the structure: a sale to a profit-maximizing buyer would compromise the customer relationship the company is built on.
- Founder-CEO continuity (Jeff Berg still CEO 18+ years in).

## Counter-evidence
Foundation-based ownership structures restrict operational flexibility for capital allocation. Planning Center's choice fits because the company is profitable and serving a stable customer base. The pattern would be harder to apply to a category that needs continuous reinvention or capital-intensive distribution. The Ministry Centered Foundation's specifics also reflect the company's faith-adjacent customer base; the same legal structure may not generalize to secular SaaS without adaptation.
