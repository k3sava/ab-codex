# What landed today — 2026-05-06

Six new cards from this week's operator pulse, two new operators, and two themes that connected from opposite directions:

## Theme 1 — Agent learning needs trace + feedback together

Harrison Chase (LangChain, May 5) and Eugene Yan (eugeneyan.com, May 3) reached the same conclusion the same week, from opposite ends of the stack. Chase from frameworks: "A trace tells you what happened. It does not, by itself, tell you whether what happened was good." Yan from evals: "Close the feedback loop — mine session transcripts for patterns to promote into config." The gap they both name is the same: most production agent stacks log traces cleanly and treat feedback as an afterthought, so improvements never compound.

- [`ins_traces-need-feedback-to-learn`](insights/ins_traces-need-feedback-to-learn.md) — Harrison Chase
- [`ins_mine-transcripts-to-promote-config`](insights/ins_mine-transcripts-to-promote-config.md) — Eugene Yan

## Theme 2 — Judgement is the part that doesn't compress

Karpathy at Sequoia AI Ascent (Apr 30) and Kevin Indig in Growth Memo (May 4) framed the same shift from technical and growth angles. Karpathy: "You can outsource your thinking, but you can't outsource your understanding." Indig: production cost collapsed but distribution shrank too, so judgement-layer work (which audience, which positioning, which investment) is now the scarce step. Verna's Stripe Sessions takeaway sits in the same space — she names pricing as a place where judgement compounds when you treat it as iterable product, not strategic decision.

- [`ins_judgment-vs-understanding`](insights/ins_judgment-vs-understanding.md) — Andrej Karpathy
- [`ins_judgment-doesnt-compress`](insights/ins_judgment-doesnt-compress.md) — Kevin Indig
- [`ins_ungate-ai-iterate-pricing`](insights/ins_ungate-ai-iterate-pricing.md) — Elena Verna

## One more — context as the leverage point in agents

Maja Voje + Benjamin Gibert published the cleanest framing of why context, not architecture, is the leverage point for agent quality: "Simple agents reading rich, specific context will outperform complex agents reading thin context every time." This is the operator's answer to the question "where do I spend the next hour to make my agent better." Spend it on the corpus.

- [`ins_rich-context-beats-sophisticated-agents`](insights/ins_rich-context-beats-sophisticated-agents.md) — Maja Voje
