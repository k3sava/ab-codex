# Elena Verna — 10 growth tactics that never work

## Summary
Elena is one of the cleanest signal sources in B2B growth. The earned-channel argument, experimentation-paralysis warning, and optionality > titles thesis are the load-bearing threads.

## Full text source
Transcript at `lenny-dropbox/Elena Verna 3.0.txt`.

## 11 load-bearing insights

1. **Don't hire a head of growth too early. Founder-led growth carries the company through PMF.** "There's a huge misconception that in order to get growth going, you need a growth team. Some companies don't even create growth teams until they're $100-200M in ARR." Two pre-conditions: (a) solid PMF with retention proof + Sean Ellis "can't live without" score, (b) data volume sufficient for experimentation. Substrate is amplifier, not creator.

2. **Don't hire a head of growth to fix a declining business.** "If you have core product and core marketing issues, growth team will not be able to fix them for you... growth can amplify great PMF; if you're slowing down, growth is helpless."

3. **Rebrands and homepage redesigns never produce performance lift.** "Never ever once have I seen a rebrand or redesign produce good performance results." Best case = net neutral, then 3-6 months of optimization to recover. The CMO-style "fresh brand" reflex is described as personal-taste injection.

4. **Stop copying competitors.** "Copying competition is the fastest way to mediocrity. You'll never be a leader if you copied somebody else." Use as inspiration substrate (web archives, signup flows, pricing pages saved in a Miro board), but never as solution. Benchmarks especially dangerous — definitions vary so widely that "signup conversion" numbers are not comparable.

5. **Your problem is not unique. Hire advisors.** "I am 99% sure your problem has been felt by somebody somewhere... not having an advisor is a growth team that is underperforming." Vetting rule: do a paid workshop first; any cagey advisor is the wrong advisor; review monthly.

6. **Earned channels > algorithm channels. Make Google richer last.** "When you're doing organic search or paid search, you're making Google richer." Earned channels = product-led virality, sharing loops, user-generated content, referral mechanics. Dropbox's sharing loop = 50% of acquisition for 17 years and counting. With AI eating the search UI, "your cost of acquisition is only going to go up... you're constantly going to be praying to algorithm gods."

7. **Add a new growth model every 18 months.** Andrew Chen's "Law of Shitty Click-throughs": over-optimizing a single loop has minimal returns. Most loops decay over 5-7 years; some (Dropbox sharing) don't. Allocate 20-25% of growth team's annual time to introducing new loops with no metric expectation for the first 12-18 months.

8. **Stop testing everything. Experimentation paralysis is a disease.** "If we cannot collect the sample size in a month, we shouldn't test it. Period." Default to pre-vs-post readouts (24h, 7d, 28d, 1yr). Reserve scientific A/B for high-traffic real estate or strategic pivots.

9. **Career optionality > titles.** "Setting a title as your goal is a very wrong way to think about it." Goal = expand the option pool annually. Evaluate every move on whether it grows or shrinks options. Full-time is one menu item; freelance, fractional, interim, advisory, courses, newsletters are all on the menu.

10. **Earn the right to optionality through full-time depth first.** "You have to earn your right to unlock optionality. That right does usually lie within full-time jobs." Counter-balance to insight 9 — don't bounce logo-to-logo with no real receipts; the optionality is unlocked by depth, not breadth.

11. **Color, third-party auth, one-off emails, and "simplification" are non-growth.** Fire round of waste tactics. Blue-vs-green is a 2000s fight. Google Auth doesn't drive incrementality on most products. One email never produces lift; emails work as series only. "Simplify onboarding" is a solution masquerading as a problem — name the problem first (confusion, education gap, navigation loss), then evaluate whether simplifying solves it.

## Other notable threads

- **Insights per minute as a north star metric.** Elena tracks her own meeting density that way.
- **Race-car framework (Lenny + Dan).** Engines, fuel, turbo boosts, oil. Useful for sorting growth initiatives — not all are loops; some are turbo-boosts (a single big launch); some are oil (instrumentation, observability).
- **Adjacent user theory (Bangaly Kaba).** Optimize for users outside the ICP without expanding PMF.
- **Growth loops as the strategic frame, not funnels.** Action → reaction → action; self-contained flywheel that spins.
- **Sci-fi reading list signal.** Project Hail Mary, A Fire Upon the Deep. Operator-class reading.
- **Memes as disarm-and-connect.** "Humor is the best way to disarm people and point out very painful situations without putting anybody on defense."
