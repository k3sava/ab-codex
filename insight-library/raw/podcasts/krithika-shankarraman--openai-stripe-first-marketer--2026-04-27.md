## 12 load-bearing insights

1. **The "use case epiphany" was OpenAI's actual marketing job.** Awareness was solved at ChatGPT launch — everyone knew of it. But "I don't know what to use it for" was the bottleneck. Marketing's job became creating moments where users went "I had no idea ChatGPT could do that."

2. **DATE diagnostic framework (anti-playbook).** When founders/CEOs ask for the playbook, refuse. Instead: (D) **Diagnose** the actual problem — look at funnel state, not the request; (A) **Analyze** competitor approaches as a baseline + gap-finder; (T) **Take a different path** — differentiation is not optional; (E) **Experiment, test, validate, scale.** Replaces "we need a demand-gen hire" with "do you have a leaky bottom or a leaky top?"

3. **"Don't litigate vanity metrics — litigate pipeline + revenue."** Retool diagnosis: paid social was driving clicks but not pipeline. They killed it and doubled down on customer storytelling — because the real differentiator was their enterprise customer logos, which competitors couldn't replicate.

4. **Cheaper is a race to the bottom, especially in AI.** "As models get cheaper, being cheaper is not a durable strategy." Differentiate on novelty, values-fit, problem-space alignment — not price.

5. **Sales rotation as a marketer's discovery loop.** Stripe required all new hires to do support rotation — built customer empathy + revealed product-knowledge gaps. Krithika's first major win was building a stack-ranked backlog of landing pages from support themes ("People keep asking about subscription payments — we never tell them we do it").

6. **Brand = velocity multiplier, not velocity tax.** "Companies that value velocity actually value brand just as much — but treat them as siloed. They're interconnected. Brand consistency *empowers* speed because the org understands how to show up." Brand = product experience + support tone + recruiting tone + every touch.

7. **The 20%/80% review checkpoints.** Don't review at 99% (rubber-stamp = useless) or 100% pre-launch only. Review at 20% (strategy: who/why/rough approach) and 80% (artifact ready, still room to change). Skip the 50% review.

8. **Marketing review forum as fishbowl.** A weekly meeting OR Slack channel OR email alias open to product/eng/marketing. Why: "Osmosis. People learn through watching others get reviewed — strategy framing, audience choice, design approach, copy choice."

9. **Process empowers new hires.** "If your second-week hire can be as productive as your two-year veteran, you've designed process well." Veterans have "social capital" they can withdraw from to get reviews — that doesn't scale. Process > tribal knowledge for a hyper-growth org.

10. **Capital-M vs lowercase-m marketing.** Capital-M = the marketing function (channels, artifacts, funnel engines). Lowercase-m = "what does the company stand for? What's the founder's storyline? What's the ICP the sales team is hunting?" — a whole-company motion. Most people conflate them.

11. **Comb-shape > T-shape for modern marketers.** T-shape (deep in one pillar — brand OR demand-gen OR product marketing) is dead. Modern PMM needs analytical-comb (best-friends-with-data-science), creative-comb, technical-comb, brand-comb. AI tools collapse the cost of being multi-domain.

12. **Taste = differentiator in the AI era.** "There's going to be so much drivel generated by AI. Companies that distinguish themselves show their craft, their understanding of product + customer, and connect the two." AI-augmented effort, not AI-replaced effort. Build taste via **exposure hours** (Vercel CMO's framing) — share-channels with examples that resonated.

## Other notable threads

- **Stripe Connect zigged via reverse-RFP.** Competitors were "become-a-payfac consulting firms." Stripe ranked for the same SEO terms by writing "If you want to be a payfac, here's the secret playbook — and by the way, this is onerous, just use Stripe Connect." Differentiation pattern: meet competitors on their terms, then redirect.
- **OpenAI cold-start activation lever.** Ask "who-are-you / what-are-you-here-for" questions even though it adds friction. Recommends right product + features afterwards. Friction-as-feature for AI products.
- **Stripe Connect launch saw 40x lead volume overnight.** Krithika built a Python lead-scoring script with ChatGPT — used in production "longer than I'd care to admit." Solo PMM as forced AI-native operator.
- **Work-life blend (Claire Hughes Johnson, ex-Stripe COO):** people + product + potential. Three components for choosing where to work. Not work-life balance — blend.
- **Pricing for AI products = experiment.** No playbook. Retool example: opening up self-hosted to non-enterprise (previously sales-gated) blew up the funnel + freed sales for higher ACV deals. The trade-off was wrenching. AI pricing especially is wild-west — seat-based, usage-based, hours-saved-based, agent-completion-based — no canonical answer yet.
- **Recommended book:** April Dunford's *Obviously Awesome* on positioning.
- **Life motto:** "The delta between expectations and reality is the function for unhappiness. It's easier to change expectations than reality." → Set expectations relentlessly, internally + externally.
