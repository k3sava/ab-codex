# Ingredients for building a successful developer PLG motion

Calyx Substack interview with Rahul Chhabria. Verbatim claims:

- "At Sentry, we build for the individual developer. Internally, we make it very clear that if we have 1 dollar to spend, we spend that whole dollar on the developer."
- Three-axis community selection: "we look at three things — product strength, community growth, and competitive advantage." Sentry mapped SDK adoption, time-to-active and tenure to identify JavaScript, Python, and PHP as strongest communities, then matched event/newsletter/podcast investment to those.
- Onboarding compression: for Next.js, "it went from 5 lines to one single command that will even create a brand new account if you don't have one." Paired with cheeky Reddit display ads — "telling developers not to click the ads to keep our CPC costs low and just run the command instead."
- "Align your product strengths to strong communities and you'll see the most growth — a spray-and-pray approach won't work."
- PMM scope at Sentry: "Our PMMs work with sales, but they're not sitting there side by side, constantly building sales enablement materials. They're working with digital marketing to figure out what we need to rank for, what content we need to create by funnel stage, and partnering with DevRel on top of funnel pieces... what we do feels more like product management than enterprise product marketing but with more focus on education."
- Two-metric scoreboard: "We look at two things, new customer acquisition and retention. I feel with a self-serve business, that's where we become the most valuable. I try to tell the team that we provide contextual education."
