# Excerpt (from miniu digest 2026-04-28)

Lily Ray published an AI-written post claiming a fake Google core update for January 2026. Within weeks, AI Overviews started confirming the fake update and citing her site as the only source.

The mechanism: AI Overviews and similar synthesis engines have a thin verification layer when a claim has limited corpus footprint, so a single seeded fake claim can self-confirm. The risk for any brand: a competitor (or random actor) seeds a hallucinated claim about deliverability, compliance, pricing, or capability; AI Overviews amplify it; remediation is reactive and slow.

Operating implication: weekly tracer-claim probes (publish a low-signal verifiable claim, watch which AI surfaces pick it up and how they cite it) become a real escalation lever for the AEO/PR function.
