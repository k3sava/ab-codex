Rewind. Backup and recovery platform for SaaS data (Jira, Confluence, GitHub, and others), repositioning in 2026 as a SaaS Resilience Platform for the age of AI agents. Their core claim: recovery speed determines AI adoption confidence, not agent capability. Rewind's survey-based evidence (27-day median restore time) and their "chilling effect" frame give the repositioning a specific diagnostic hook rather than a general fear appeal.

## Operating themes
- **Recovery as adoption prerequisite** AI agent adoption ceilings are set by recovery infrastructure confidence, not by what agents can do
- **Narrative timing** Product repositioning tied to visible market fear cycles (high-profile agent incidents) accelerates category formation
